Freezing Térere

https://www.youtube.com/live/-LLFHdOoXpE?si=sbtAsp7SH5hWHz9m&t=5365

This is a rework of Sipping on Térere (2019), which was also composed for brass quintet (substituting Euphonium for Trumpet 2)

By far the most challenging aspect of this work was creating the real-time audio reactive visuals, which required me to learn GLSL,

the shading language utilized within the OpenGL graphics rendering pipeline. In my view, this is the most feasible way of performing video

synthesis using computers since it is extremely performative, platform-independent and runs directly on the graphics card. While learning

the language and how to embed it within my broader C++ environment was quite difficult, it was undoubtably necessary to my future research

and work. I already find it easier than Jitter (within Max) which is the video synthesis tool I have the next most experience with.

It also embedds much more nicely into C++, my preferred audio programming environment, whereas using something like Jitter would

probably necessitate OSC messages to control everything. GLSL is also exponentially more open ended as a programming tool.

I think I spent at least five times as long on the visuals as the music for this work and they probably consumed about half of my total

independent composition time over the course of the Fall 2023 semester.
